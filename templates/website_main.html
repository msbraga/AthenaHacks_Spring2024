RECAPIFY

By Rachel Appenzeller, Marina Braga and Alexandra Klimko

About the program

Recapify is a program that records a lecture and returns a summary of what was said.

We had this idea, because one of the members of the group is an international student at the University of Southern California and when she was a freshman she had difficulty understanding the fast passed classes in English as English is not her first language. She saw that if Recapify existed back then she would have adapted way easier

Applications

This application can be used by any student, but it also can be so much more, for example it can help several people that are affected by a disability to succeed in class

If someone is hard of hearing they can have access to a written summ up of what was talked about in class. Another example is people that have Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD). Those people have difficulty regulating their attention span, so instead of forcing themselves to concentrate for a long period of time in class, they can just read a summed up version
